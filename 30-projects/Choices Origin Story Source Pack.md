# Choices Origin Story Source Pack

Canonical story material for every product-facing telling of "why Choices exists." One source, many surfaces — see Usage map. Promote to `30-projects/` once the gap interview is filled.

**Test for done:** could a stranger draft Post #1 from this note alone, without inventing anything?

## Beats we have (canonical text + source)

| Beat | Canonical text | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **The ritual** | The couple *races to say "choices" first*. The loser has to give 4 options; the winner can't complain about the result. "We've done this for years. So I built it into an app." | [[Choices Growth Plan]] §9 (ritual insight); [[Choices Marketing Proposal]] Teaser 2 script |
| **The pain** | The Loop: "What do you want to eat?" / "I don't know… whatever you want." / "I picked last time." POV: it's 6:47pm. | [[Choices Marketing Proposal]] Teaser 1; positioning ("end The Loop without a fight") |
| **The insight** | Nobody picks the winner — you take turns *cutting* until one survives. No blame (nobody owns the outcome), no apathy (everyone acted). Elimination, not selection. | [[Studio Design Constitution]] rubric; proposal positioning ("play, don't spin") |
| **The name story** | A full rename exploration (Choosy ⭐, Choicey, Picky, Dibs, Snackdown…) died on partner review — directions felt "too hard/corporate." Locked decision: keep "Choices." The name has emotional equity with its first two users; the wife is attached to it and holds the veto. The fix was voice, not name. | [[Choices Growth Plan]] §9 naming table + decision (2026-07-01) |
| **Design as love letter** | Streaks count *play days*, not wins — the game deliberately has no per-player winner, so there's nothing to hold over each other. Never paywall the join. Nothing screenshottable into a fight. The reveal: "Nobody picked this. Everybody picked this." | [[Studio Design Constitution]] ethic; [[Choices Growth Plan]] streak design |
| **Timeline** | Built solo; v1 live at choices.austinjuliuskim.com June 2026; wife = first user; "wife-approval of the native feel" is the literal iOS validation gate. | [[Choices Growth Plan]] status + §3 |
| **The category claim** | Not a food app — a couples game. Competitors are spin-the-wheel utilities; this is play. | [[Choices Marketing Proposal]] positioning |

## Gap interview (fill before drafting Post #1)

Only Austin/his wife can answer these. Do not invent; mark pending.

- [ ] **1. Ritual origin** — when/how did the race-to-say-"choices" start? One specific remembered instance (where were you, who lost, what was dinner?).
- [ ] **2. The worst Loop moment** — the concrete hangry-evening story that makes a reader feel it (the fight, the 45 minutes, the cereal-for-dinner ending).
- [ ] **3. First real decision** — the first actual dinner the app decided: what won, what she said when it did.
- [ ] **4. Her v1 reaction** — first impression, anything she vetoed or made better (there's already one on record: the naming-direction rejection).
- [ ] **5. Her comfort level** *(gates everything)* — how much of her can be in the story? Per [[Choices Marketing Proposal]]: nothing shot or published with her until she's genuinely enthusiastic; forced participation reads false.
- [ ] **6. Artifacts** — early screenshots, the first join code you two used, texts from the build week, the git commit that shipped v1.
