# Web App Client No. 1

First paid client engagement, business-development / pre-contract phase. Target ≥$400 revenue to establish profit motive. Implementation work is explicitly out of scope for this doc — it becomes a separate project after contract signature (see [[Web App Client No. 1 Execution Harness]] row H → Handoff).

**Client:** Margie — owner of Je T'adore Pet Salon & Bakery (Montrose, CA) and California Dulce Doodles (Labradoodle breeder, Glendale).

## Audit findings (2026-07-23)

- **jetadorepetsalon.com** — already on GoDaddy Website Builder 8.0. Needs SEO/content tune-up only: title tag is "JPS - Home", stale seasonal notices, typos.
- **californiadulcedoodles.com** — WordPress 5.8.13, unpatched since ~2022 (security liability), `/index.php/` permalinks, mixed-content warnings, broken social links. This is the rescue/migration candidate.

## Strategy

Consolidate both sites to a managed platform (GoDaddy or Squarespace — decide after client input on the discovery call). Custom stack rejected: conflicts with the one-time-engagement / no-ongoing-webmaster constraint the client needs.

## Pricing

- Market packages observed: $600–$4,500.
- Recommended anchor: "Rescue & Rebuild" at $2,000–$3,000.

## Entity decision rule

- Start date < 3 weeks out → invoice as sole proprietor.
- Start date further out → parallel-file a CA LLC before invoicing.

## Discovery call plan

Call guide covers: trigger question, current-spend inventory, credential check, scope probes, budget calibration ($1.5–4.5K), soft close.

## Proposal & contract shape

- **Proposal:** 1–2 pages, plain language, before/after framing, 2–3 fixed-price options, no-retainer positioning, hourly rate card for future asks.
- **Contract:** fixed price, 2 revision rounds, client-provides-content clause, IP transfers on final payment, explicit no-maintenance-obligation language, 30-day warranty. Peter Park sanity check optional before client signature.
